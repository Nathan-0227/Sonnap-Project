# Home Page Design

## Literature-Based Design

The Sonnap Home Page is designed based on behavioral science, gamification, and digital health intervention theories. Rather than simply displaying sleep data, the Home Page aims to encourage users to develop healthier sleep habits through goal setting, motivation, emotional engagement, and positive behavioral reinforcement.

---

## 1. Sleep Goal Setting

### Literature Support

**Bedtime Procrastination: Introducing a New Area of Procrastination**

Kroese, F. M., De Ridder, D. T., Evers, C., & Adriaanse, M. A. (2014)

### Key Findings

The study introduced the concept of **Bedtime Procrastination**, which refers to failing to go to bed at the intended time without external reasons preventing sleep.

Researchers found that bedtime procrastination is strongly associated with poor self-regulation and is a major contributor to insufficient sleep.

### Application in Sonnap

The Home Page allows users to set a target bedtime (e.g., 23:30) to increase awareness of their sleep schedule and reduce bedtime procrastination.

**Features:**

* Sleep goal setting
* Bedtime reminders
* Sleep schedule awareness

### Reference

https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2014.00611/full

## 2. Mission System

### Literature Support

**Gamification for Health and Wellbeing: A Systematic Review of the Literature**

Johnson, D., Deterding, S., Kuhn, K. A., Staneva, A., Stoyanov, S., & Hides, L. (2016)

### Key Findings

Gamification techniques such as challenges, rewards, achievements, and progress tracking can effectively increase user engagement and motivation in health-related behavior change.

### Application in Sonnap

The Mission System transforms healthy sleep behaviors into achievable daily tasks and goals.

**Features:**

* Daily sleep missions
* Reward-based progression
* Habit formation support

### Reference

https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214782916300620

## 3. Virtual Pet Companion

### Literature Support

**Designing for Healthy Lifestyles: Design Considerations for Mobile Technologies to Encourage Consumer Health and Wellness**

Consolvo, S., McDonald, D. W., et al. (2008)

### Key Findings

Research suggests that metaphorical visualizations and virtual companions can create emotional attachment and increase long-term adherence to healthy behaviors.

Users are more motivated to maintain positive habits when they feel responsible for a virtual companion.

### Application in Sonnap

The Virtual Pet represents the user's sleep health condition.

Healthy sleep habits help the pet grow and remain happy, while poor sleep habits may negatively affect the pet's condition.

**Features:**

* Virtual pet growth
* Emotional attachment
* Long-term motivation

### Reference

https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1357054.1357121

## 4. Sleep Score Feedback

### Literature Support

**Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness**

Thaler, R. H., & Sunstein, C. R. (2008)

### Key Findings

Nudge Theory proposes that small changes in decision environments can encourage people to make healthier choices without restricting freedom of choice.

Providing clear and immediate feedback can increase behavioral awareness and improve decision-making.

### Application in Sonnap

The Sleep Score system provides users with an easy-to-understand evaluation of their sleep quality and encourages continuous improvement.

**Features:**

* Sleep score visualization
* Positive feedback messages
* Behavioral awareness

### Reference

https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300262285/nudge/

The Home Page combines behavioral science, gamification, emotional engagement, and digital nudging strategies to encourage healthier sleep habits and reduce bedtime procrastination.
